\section{Model 3: Feature Attribution and Mechanism Design}
\label{sec:model3}

\noindent\textbf{\textit{This section addresses Tasks 3--4:}} We quantify how contestant attributes influence survival, then design a dynamic weighting mechanism guided by a Pareto frontier.

\subsection{Feature Attribution with XGBoost + SHAP}

We predict contestant survival weeks using XGBoost and explain the model using SHAP values. Features include judge-score statistics, celebrity age, industry, and a top-$K$ professional partner bucket.

\noindent\textbf{Key insight:} Technical performance (judge statistics) is the strongest driver, followed by professional partner effects; age contributes nonlinearly rather than monotonically.

\subsection{Dynamic Adaptive Weighting}

We propose a dynamic rule that shifts weight from judges to fans as the season progresses:
\begin{equation}
\alpha_w =
\begin{cases}
\alpha_0, & w \le 5,\\
\alpha_0 - (\alpha_0-\alpha_T)\dfrac{w-5}{W-5}, & w > 5,
\end{cases}
\end{equation}
with $(\alpha_0,\alpha_T)=(0.7,0.4)$.

    \caption{\textbf{Dynamic Adaptive Weighting Schedule.} Early weeks emphasize judges; later weeks prioritize audience intent.}
    \label{fig:dynamic_schedule}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Pareto Frontier}

We evaluate static weights across $\alpha \in [0.3,0.8]$ and compute two objectives: \textit{judge alignment} (fairness) and \textit{fan influence}. \figref{fig:pareto} shows the trade-off.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.75\textwidth]{figures/fig_pareto_frontier.pdf}
    \caption{\textbf{Pareto Frontier for Mechanism Design.} Each point is a different $\alpha$. The 60/40 rule yields judge alignment $\approx 0.50$ and fan influence $\approx 0.83$.}
    \label{fig:pareto}
\end{figure}

\noindent\textbf{Recommendation:} Use dynamic weighting to preserve early technical screening while maintaining late-stage audience ownership.
