\section{Model 3: Mechanism Design}
\label{sec:model3}

We address: \textbf{Can we design a voting mechanism that improves fairness while maintaining engagement?}

\subsection{Operationalizing Fairness}

We define fairness using two independent dimensions:
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.1em]
    \item \textbf{Technical Fairness:} Eliminated contestant has judge percentage below median.
    \item \textbf{Popularity Fairness:} Eliminated contestant has inferred fan support in lower half (using interval bounds, not point estimates).
\end{itemize}

A \textbf{``wrongful elimination''} occurs when both conditions fail---i.e., the eliminated contestant had above-median support on \emph{both} dimensions.

\subsection{Candidate Mechanisms}

\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=0.1em]
    \item \textbf{Soft Floor:} $C_i^{\text{floor}} = \max(C_i, \theta)$ --- guarantees minimum score.
    \item \textbf{Weighted Percent (Proposed):} 
\end{enumerate}
\begin{equation}
    C_i^{\text{new}} = \alpha \cdot J_i^{\%} + (1-\alpha) \cdot v_i, \quad J_i^{\%} = \frac{J_i}{\sum_k J_k},
\end{equation}
where $\alpha = 0.6$. This is \emph{not} Borda (which uses ranks); we use normalized percentages.

\subsection{Counterfactual Simulation}

We replay all 230 elimination rounds under each mechanism:

\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \caption{Mechanism Comparison}
    \label{tab:mechanism_comparison}
    \begin{tabular}{lccc}
        \toprule
        \textbf{Mechanism} & \textbf{Param} & \textbf{Wrongful} & \textbf{Rate} \\
        \midrule
        Current System & 50/50 & 40 & 17.4\% \\
        Soft Floor & $\theta=5\%$ & 94 & 40.9\% \\
        \textbf{Weighted Percent} & $\alpha=0.6$ & \textbf{3} & \textbf{1.3\%} \\
        \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
\end{table}

\noindent\textbf{Key Finding:} Soft Floor \emph{backfires} (94 wrongful); Weighted Percent ($\alpha=0.6$) achieves \textbf{92.5\% reduction}.

\subsection{Implementation Recommendation}

We recommend $\alpha = 0.6$ Weighted Percent: (1) 92.5\% fairness gain, (2) transparent ``60\% skill, 40\% popularity,'' (3) robust across $\alpha \in [0.5, 0.7]$.
