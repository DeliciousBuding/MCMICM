\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\subsection{Summary of Findings}

This paper developed a comprehensive framework for inferring fan votes from \emph{Dancing with the Stars} elimination data. Our key findings:

\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=0.2em]
    \item \textbf{Dual-Core Inversion:} Successfully reconstructed fan vote distributions for 32 of 34 seasons using hybrid LP/CP with 100\% constraint satisfaction and median bound width of 8.3\%.
    
    \item \textbf{Anomaly Detection:} Identified S32/S33 as ``hard anomalies'' where no feasible fan vote exists under standard logic, providing an early warning system for voting irregularities.
    
    \item \textbf{Professional Dancer Effect:} Survival analysis reveals professional partner explains \textbf{42.9\%} of variance---larger than celebrity type (8.2\%) or prior fame (3.7\%).
    
    \item \textbf{Weighted Borda Count:} Proposed mechanism ($0.6J + 0.4V$) reduces wrongful eliminations by \textbf{92.5\%}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Recommendations}

\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=0.2em]
    \item \textbf{Pre-Broadcast Checks:} Run LP/CP solver before broadcast; flag $S^* > 0.5$.
    \item \textbf{Adopt Weighted Borda:} $\text{Score}_i = 0.6 \widetilde{J}_i + 0.4 \widetilde{V}_i$.
    \item \textbf{Diversify Dancer Pool:} Rotate professionals or balance historical win rates.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Limitations}

\begin{itemize}[itemsep=0.1em]
    \item Unobserved heterogeneity (social media campaigns, news events).
    \item Weaker bounds for rank-only seasons (S28--S34).
    \item Cannot determine anomaly \emph{cause} (only detection).
    \item Counterfactual assumes unchanged voting behavior.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Future Work}

Future extensions: (1) real-time dashboard for producers, (2) international expansion to \emph{Strictly Come Dancing} and other franchises, (3) incorporating social media sentiment to tighten bounds.

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent\textbf{Key Takeaway:} The current DWTS system produces 17.4\% wrongful eliminations. Our Weighted Borda reduces this to 1.3\%---a \textbf{92.5\% improvement}---while maintaining viewer engagement.
